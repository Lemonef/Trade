Validation · Discretionary framework

Backtest Spike

Can't backtest a discretionary catalyst framework mechanically — so this is a blind walkthrough: pick real past setups, enrich each with an as-of-date catalyst packet (ticker masked, future hidden), score /6 + ENTER/WATCH/DROP blind, then reveal the forward outcome. Metric = tradeable first-touch +40% target before −25% stop over 365d (PEAK = max gain in window, shown as context). Tool: tools/blind_walkthrough.py in SecondBrain.

⚠️ Promising but NOT validated. A real, replicated entry-edge signal across 2 independent batches — but imperfect blinding (enricher knew the ticker), hand-built/era-biased pool, n<50, and zero live realized track record. Honest label: keep paper-trading; don't bet real money yet.

3 · Findings

  • Entry judgment is real. ENTER cases have the highest capture AND by far the highest forward peaks — replicated across two batches. The /6 picks the winners.
  • Price-only is anti-signal. A chart-only blind test (no catalyst) was ~anti-predictive — proving the edge is the catalyst judgment, not chart-reading.
  • ★ Don't over-manage the winners. On the monster spikes, mechanical trim/trail gives up ~700pp of return to save ~30pp of drawdown. Management must be asymmetric: small disaster-stop + thesis-break exit on losers, but LET WINNERS RUN on the ones working.
  • Scale-in beats all-in. Starter-⅓-then-add-on-confirmation beat full-deploy-at-entry on BOTH return (+6pp) and drawdown (−24pp) — validating the existing sizing discipline.
  • WATCH is the noisy bucket (~coin-flip) — tighten it or push more into DROP.

4 · To close the gap to "validated"

  • Re-score the ENTER bucket on an asymmetric run-the-winners exit (done — see §2).
  • One more batch → n≥50 with a separate blind judge (enricher ≠ judge) to kill the blinding caveat.
  • Build the pool from an as-of-date screener (not a hand-picked list) to kill selection bias.
  • Accrue a live realized track record (the forward verdict ledger) — the only unimpeachable proof.
